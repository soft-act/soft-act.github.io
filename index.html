Functional Force-Aware Retargeting from Virtual Human Demos to Soft Robot Policies

Abstract

We introduce SoftAct, a framework for teaching soft robot hands to perform human-like manipulation skills by explicitly reasoning about contact forces. Leveraging immersive virtual reality, our system captures rich demonstrations including hand kinematics, object motion, dense contact patches, and detailed contact force information. SoftAct uses a two-stage force-aware retargeting strategy: a force-balanced finger assignment stage and an online contact-informed refinement stage with geodesic weighting. We evaluate SoftAct on contact-rich manipulation tasks with a non-anthropomorphic pneumatic soft hand. The method improves low-level tracking quality, object-level trajectory fidelity, and policy success in both simulation and zero-shot real-world deployment.

Method

Two-Stage Retargeting

  • Stage 1: Force-balanced finger assignment. Demonstrated contact forces are attributed to human fingers and redistributed to robot fingers to construct a force-balanced mapping.
  • Stage 2: Contact-informed refinement. During execution, fingertip targets are adjusted online using geodesic-weighted contact influence to improve contact consistency under morphology mismatch.

Low-Level Soft-Hand Control

SoftAct uses a learned low-level controller to convert desired fingertip displacements into pneumatic chamber pressures. This improves precision and stability for nonlinear soft-finger actuation and supports robust policy rollout.

Evaluation & Results

Key Results from the Paper

  • Low-level soft-finger trajectory tracking RMSE is reduced by up to 55% versus the strongest baseline, with variance reduced by up to 69%.
  • Object trajectory tracking in simulation shows substantial gains, with translational error reductions of roughly 55-75% and rotational improvements up to 80% versus kinematic retargeting.
  • In simulation, policy success improves strongly across tasks, including: paper cup pouring 90% vs 40%, box reorienting 90% vs 30%, and light bulb insertion 47% vs 0%.
  • In zero-shot real-world deployment, SoftAct outperforms kinematic baselines: paper cup pouring 85% vs 35%, light bulb screwing 95% vs 30%, and box reorienting 70% vs 10%.
